Analysis and Visualization Report: WeRateDogs¶

Data¶

After cleaning the data, it was stored in a csv file named "twitter_archive_master.csv". All of the data for this analysis was loaded into pandas dataframe by loading this file.

Analysis and Visualization¶

Questions¶

The most important thing in analyzing data is to ask questions. So, the questions for which i tried to find answers were:

  • Is there any correlation between Retweets and Favourite?
  • What is the total number of Retweets and Favourite for a given year?
  • Are they continously increasing for a given year?
  • What is the total number of Retweets and Favourite for a given month?
  • Are they continously increasing for a months?
  • Which stage of the dog have most and least Retweet count.
  • Which stage of the dog have most and least Favourite count.
  • Which is the most famous dog breed by tweet count.

Strong Relationship¶

By analyzing the figure below, we can observe that the retweet count and favourite count are highly correlated. It means that if the retweets are high, then there is a high possibility of the favourite count also, being high. The results are according to the expectations due to the apparent high chances of it. Plotting retweet count and favourite count using scatter plot provides us with these results.

plt.scatter(df['favorite_count'], df['retweet_count'],color='C2');

Results according to year¶

By viewing the plot below, we can say that the number of favourite count is continously increasing but the retweet count reached at it's maximum point, in the year 2016 and then started to decrease. But, this only shows the result according to a given year, let's get more insight by viewing it according to months.

In [4]:
retweet_year = df.groupby('year')['retweet_count'].sum()
favorite_year = df.groupby('year')['favorite_count'].sum()
plt.figure(figsize=(8,4), dpi = 80)
plt.xlabel('Retweets by year')
plt.ylabel('Retweets Count')
plt.plot(retweet_year,label='Retweets')
plt.plot(favorite_year,label='Favorite')
plt.legend()
plt.show()

Results according to month¶

We can see that the favourite count is always more then the retweet count and it is according to the expectations as more people would like to just click rather than retweeting. We also get an insight from it, that is, the favourite and retweet count are more likely to increase in the month with holidays. For instance, we can see that the plot reaches it's peak value in the month of July in which people have summer holidays and in the month of December and January when people have winter holidays.

Popularity by Retweet Count¶

The two diagrams below show us that that "Doggo" dog stage have the most retweets count and "Pupper" dog stage have the most favourite counts, making these two the most popular dog stage and the "Floofer" dog stage have got the least amount of retweets and favourite counts. But, this fact should also be considered that there is a possibility of tweets related to 'Floofer' dog stage being less in number than those of 'Pupper" dog stage; hence, as a result, it can be assumed that the total amount of retweet and favourite count being lower or high than others, could be a result of this unequal count.

Popular Dog Breed¶

The next analysis is about the most famous dog breed by tweet count. unfortunately, the data that we got after feeding through the neural network, to classify the breed of the dogs, didn't predict all the images as dogs. There were some images on which it was misclassified so, for my analysis, I didn't include the records with misclassifications. In my analysis, I have displayed the popularity of 15 most famous breeds, according to the tweet count, and we can see that "golden retriever" is the most famous dog breed with a count close to 140.
